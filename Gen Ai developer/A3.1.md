# A3.1 — Word2Vec Training & Embedding Visualization

**Phase:** 3 — Natural Language Processing  
**Topic:** Core NLP, Word Embeddings  
**Type:** Assignment

---

## Goal
Train Word2Vec embeddings on a custom text corpus and explore the semantic relationships they capture. Visualize clusters with t-SNE.

---

## Task

### Part 1 — Corpus Preparation
- Download a plain text corpus (e.g., Wikipedia dump subset, a collection of news articles, or 5 books from Project Gutenberg)
- Preprocess: lowercase, remove punctuation, tokenize into sentences then words
- Minimum corpus size: 1 million words

### Part 2 — Train Word2Vec
Using `gensim.models.Word2Vec`:
- Train Skip-gram model: `sg=1`, `vector_size=100`, `window=5`, `min_count=5`, `epochs=10`
- Also train CBOW: `sg=0` with same settings
- Save both models

### Part 3 — Nearest Neighbors
For 10 query words of your choice (e.g., "king", "computer", "doctor"), find the top 5 most similar words in both models. Print a comparison table.

### Part 4 — Analogy Test
Test word analogies using vector arithmetic:
- `king - man + woman = ?` → should be close to "queen"
- Test 5 such analogies
- Report which ones work and which fail

### Part 5 — t-SNE Visualization
- Pick 5 semantic categories (10 words each): e.g., countries, sports, food, animals, tech
- Reduce 100-dimensional embeddings to 2D using t-SNE
- Scatter plot with words labeled and colored by category
- Do semantically related words cluster together?

---

## Deliverables
- [ ] Trained Skip-gram and CBOW models saved
- [ ] Nearest neighbors comparison table (Skip-gram vs CBOW)
- [ ] Analogy test results (5 examples)
- [ ] t-SNE scatter plot with semantic clusters
- [ ] Written observation: which model captures semantics better?
